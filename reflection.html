Week 7: Reflections


Zach Petersen

My primary role for this group was project management. I coordinated the group resources and delegated tasks to team members. I solicited feedback and engagement from to group during planning stages, and attempted to align the weekly tasks with our planned application requirements. I also inserted myself as an individual contributor by writing code for some of these tasks.

The most significant problem we encountered was lack of communication or engagement from group members. We ultimately solved this problem by no longer delegating weekly tasks to them. We wanted to provide them with ample opportunity to participate in the group, but it was coming at the cost of quality for our finished products. I believe that the 100% online collaboration setting can make it difficult to actively engage all group members.

Personally, I thought it was interesting to build and see the interaction of the different moving pieces. (HTML, SQL, PHP, JSON...) Being the in the role of project manager for the team was more challenging than I initially anticipated. Coordinating the efforts of multiple contributors remotely was not simple every week.

I think that the experience of the group work and team dynamics was more valuable than the finished web application itself for this assignment. The skills of being a quality group team member and collaborating effectively will have applications and value beyond just server-side development. The cloud 9 platform was new to me prior to this course, but I can see it being a great tool for collaborative needs in the future. I think the portability of the workspace is a novel feature, being able to create/edit and compile code from anywhere.


Andy Bowar

My primary role for this group was web master. I was responsible for getting the page up on the web server and making sure it functioned properly there.
There are also multiple points where I was responsible for writing forms and testing code that others wrote to confirm that it worked properly.

The main problems we encountered were difficulties in getting everyone to engage. We regularly did not get everything done in a week because we had people
assigned to tasks that were not around or were not starting their work until an hour before it was supposed to be finished. As a result, our site does not look
nearly as finished as it should.

My main takeaway from this project was the realization of how do-able a big project like this can be with proper delegation. When all I have to worry is my own
little part, it's much less overwhelming to consider getting it all done.

One thing I learned from working in a team was how important it is to be in constant communication. If you don't continue communicating, the group breaks down
and work does not get done properly.
